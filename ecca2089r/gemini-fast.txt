Mary Warrin, affirmd: to ye Jury of Inquest. that: she hath
been: afflicted by Giles Cory or his apparition and
that by beating of me with his staffe: & by: biting me
pinching & choaking me greatly tormenting me & cutting me
with a knife. & particularly at ye time of his examination
he did greviously torment me: also: at the time of his
examination. I saw: sd Cory or his apparition most dread
fully afflict: Mary Walcot Ann Putnam: mercy lewis &
Sarah: Vibber Septr. 9. 1692

Eliz Woodwell upon ye oath she formerly has taken in
this Court: did affirm to ye Jury of Inquest: that: she saw
Giles Cory at meeting at Salem on a lecture day. since
he has bin at prison he or his apparition: came in &
sat: in ye middlemost seat: of ye mens seats. by ye post
this was lecture day before: Bridget Bishop was hanged
and I saw him come out: with ye rest of ye people. at yt time
Mary Walcot: affirmed: yt saw sd Cory: as above: sit: in ye
same place at ye same time he or his appearance & yt she
se him goe out: with ye rest of ye people. this she affirmd
to ye Jury of Inquest. Septr 9: 1692

Eliz Hubbard: to ye Jury of Inquest. that Giles Cory hath several
times afflicted me with several sorts of torments. I verily think
he is a wizard & afflicted me by witchcraft Septr 9th: 1692
