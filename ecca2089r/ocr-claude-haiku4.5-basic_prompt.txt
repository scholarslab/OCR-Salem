Mary Warin, affirm'd to 9th June of Inquest, that she had been kick'd offended by Giles Cory or his apparition and that by kicking of me with his staff, & by biting me a pinching & choaking me, greatly tormenting the. & calling me will a knife. & particularly at ye time of his examinatn he sod grievously tormented me, also at the time of his examination. I saw D Cory or his apparition most Dreadfully afflixt. Mary Walet for Pulleram. mary iving & Sarah Vibber Septy 9.1692

Elis Woodwell upon ye oath the formerly has taken in the court, D'd affirm to ye Jury of Inquest, that she saw Giles Cory at meeting at Salem on a lecture Day, Mill he has bin at prison he or his apparition came in a sat, in ye middmost seat, of ye mens seats, by ye post the was ye Day before. By sod Busking way hanging'd and I saw him come ont with ye rest of ye people. Let me mary Walet affirmed, if this D Cory, D, above, sit in ye same place at ye same time he or his apparition & if she sod see hinger one, with ye rest of ye people, this she affirm to ye Jury of Inquest. Septy 9.1692

Eliz Hubbard to ye Jury of Inquest, that Giles Cory hath Several times afficted Seavroll Several sorts of torments, & verily, with he's a wizard & affil'd me by witchcraft Seplt 9:1692
