Mary Walcot affirmed to ye Jury of Inquest that she had been grievously afflicted by Giles Cory or his apparition and that by hurting of me with his staff & by striking me & pinching & choaking me, greatly tormenting me & cutting me with a knife. & particularly at ye time of his examination he did grievously torment me, also at the time of his examination I saw Giles Cory or his apparition most dreadfully afflict. Mary Walcot (sr) & Mary Lewis & Sarah Vibber. Sept: 9. 1692

Eliz Woodwell upon & oath the former being taken in this Court did affirm to ye Jury of Inquest that she saw Giles Cory at meeting at Salem on a Lecture day since he has bin at prison he or his apparition came & sat in ye middle most seat of women's seats by ye post this was but one day before the next Lecture day. George Bithop was hanged and I saw him come and walk ye rest of ye people at ye time Mary Walcot affirmed if this Giles Cory as above it is ye same time he or his apparance & if she did she him so out with ye rest of ye people, this she affirm'd to ye Jury of Inquest. Sept: 9. 1692

Eliz Hubbard to ye Jury of Inquest that Giles Cory hath several times afflicted tormented several sorts of torments & verily think he is a wizard & afflicted me by witchcraft. Sept: 9: 1692
